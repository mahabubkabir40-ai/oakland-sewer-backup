Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the structural drying phase take?
Depending on how long the water was standing and the porosity of the materials, a standard structural basement drying cycle takes between 3 to 5 days of continuous industrial LGR dehumidification and high-velocity air mover operation.
Why is vacuuming water not enough for a flooded basement?
Concrete, drywall, and structural framing act like sponges. Simply vacuuming surface water leaves behind deep-seated moisture inside the masonry and framing pores, which leads to structural wood rot and toxic mold outbreaks. Continuous moisture mapping and dehumidification are required.
Does homeowners insurance cover flooded basement cleanup?
Yes, standard water damage policies cover basement flooding caused by sudden plumbing bursts or sump pump overflows (with proper riders). We perform complete digital moisture logging and photo documentation to support your claim.
