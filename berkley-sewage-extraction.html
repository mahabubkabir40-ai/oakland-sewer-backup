Frequently Asked Questions

Can standard sump pumps clear raw sewage?
No, standard residential sump pumps are designed for storm water, not thick sewage sludge and organic solids, which will quickly clog and burn out the motor. Rapid sewage extraction requires heavy-duty, truck-mounted vacuum pumping systems that are designed to handle Category 3 black water safely.
What are the health risks of DIY sewage extraction?
Sewage water contains trillions of dangerous pathogens, including E. coli, Salmonella, and Hepatitis A. Skin contact or breathing aerosolized moisture can lead to severe illness. Professional technicians use specialized PPE, full containment barriers, and negative air machines to prevent cross-contamination during sewage extraction.
How is the extracted sewage disposed of?
We comply with local Oakland County environmental regulations. All extracted sewage is safely contained in truck-mounted tanks and transported to designated waste treatment facilities to protect the local environment and public health.
